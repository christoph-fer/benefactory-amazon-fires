In the past 48 hours, 2000 new fires have started in the Amazon despite the President’s ban on deliberate burning.

Donate now to put power in the hands of people at the front lines of forest protection in the Amazon.


When Jair Bolsonaro – a climate change sceptic - got elected as Brazil’s President, he promised to roll back environmental and indigenous protections and open the Amazon rainforest to industry to boost the economy.


74,000 fires have been recorded this year in the rainforest, threatening its future, the billions of plants and animals, and possibly the entire planet’s health.


20% of the world’s oxygen is produced by the Amazon. If more of the Amazon is destroyed, it would not only stop producing this oxygen and supporting this wildlife, but it would create a feedback loop worsening climate change.
